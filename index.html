Frequently Asked Questions

What is Soenlis?

Soenlis is an intelligent web security scanner, designed for the Raise Your Hack 2025 hackathon. It utilizes an advanced multi-agent architecture and AI to identify vulnerabilities, detect web technologies, and generate detailed audit reports. Its goal is to provide precise and rapid security assessments for developers, pentesters, businesses, and students.

Why is Soenlis a "Best Use of Coral Protocol"?

Soenlis excels in its use of Coral Protocol by leveraging its ability to orchestrate a team of specialized AI agents. Rather than a monolithic approach, Soenlis breaks down the audit into tasks managed by dedicated agents (Orchestrator, Reconnaissance, CVE Auditor, Report Generator), each communicating securely and efficiently via Coral. This demonstrates Coral's power for decentralized collaboration, modularity, and scalability in a complex cybersecurity system, embodying the very essence of innovation sought by Coral Protocol.

What technologies does Soenlis use?

Soenlis is built on a modern technology stack including:

  • Coral Protocol: For orchestration and communication between AI agents.
  • Groq API & Llama 3.1: For accelerating AI inferences and intelligent data analysis.
  • Python: Primary language for the backend and agent logic.
  • Vultr: For scalable cloud deployment of our infrastructure.
  • Wappalyzer & CVE Databases: For technology detection and known vulnerability lookups.
  • HTML/CSS (TailwindCSS) & JavaScript: For the web user interface and interactions.

How does Soenlis ensure scan security?

Soenlis operates in an ethical mode, prioritizing non-intrusiveness. Our system is designed to collect passive information and analyze public data or data accessible via non-destructive methods. Coral Protocol's multi-agent architecture also ensures that communications between our components are secure and resilient. Scans focus on detection and reporting, without active exploitation of vulnerabilities.
